Wedding catering works best when it balances atmosphere with practicality. A relaxed sharing menu may sound perfect, but it still needs to work for timings, staffing, and guest needs.

Gather dietary information early and give your caterer a clean, organised list. That makes it much easier to avoid last-minute changes and ensures guests with allergies or specific requirements are looked after properly.

You should also think about the full day of food and drink, not just the wedding breakfast. Arrival drinks, evening food, and water stations all make a difference to how comfortable people feel.
