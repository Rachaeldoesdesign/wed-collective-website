A wedding timeline should support the mood of the day, not fight it. Guests notice when there are long gaps, confusing transitions, or several things competing for attention at once.

Work backwards from fixed points such as ceremony time, sunset, food service, and music finish times. Then leave breathing room between each section so suppliers are not forced into unrealistic handovers.

If you are unsure where to be generous, put the extra time around travel, group photographs, and room turnarounds. Those are the moments most likely to shift slightly on the day.
