Great entertainment can carry the whole second half of your wedding, but it works best when the logistics are sorted early.

Ask about set lengths, breaks, sound limits, power requirements, and arrival times before you book. Those details matter just as much as the playlist when the venue has tight turnaround windows or noise restrictions.

It is also worth being clear about the mood you want. A packed dance floor can mean very different things depending on whether you imagine sing-alongs, soul classics, indie favourites, or a club-style finish.
